# Raw Source Note - Official TCF Canada Source Check

Access date: 2026-05-17.

## FEI TCF Canada

URL: https://www.france-education-international.fr/test/tcf-canada

Confirmed for implementation:

- Four mandatory sections evaluate general French: listening, reading, writing, speaking.
- Listening: 39 multiple-choice questions, 35 minutes.
- Reading: 39 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes.
- Writing: 3 tasks, 60 minutes.
- Speaking: 3 tasks, 12 minutes including 2 minutes of preparation.
- Total duration is listed as 2 hours 47.
- Listening recordings are played once.
- Writing word limits are Task 1 60-120 words, Task 2 120-150 words, Task 3 120-180 words.
- TCF Canada can be retaken with a 30-day wait between sittings.
- TCF attestation validity is 2 years from issue date.
- FEI recommends RFI and TV5MONDE practice resources.

## FEI Evaluation

URL: https://www.france-education-international.fr/article/evaluation-epreuves-tcf

Confirmed for implementation:

- QCM scores are corrected centrally and transformed with a barème that considers item difficulty.
- FEI does not communicate candidates' raw number of correct QCM answers.
- Speaking and writing are evaluated independently by trained raters.
- Evaluation criteria include linguistic, pragmatic, and sociolinguistic dimensions.
- Computer-based writing may receive a first automated correction aid, but a human second correction remains.

## IRCC Express Entry Language Results

URL: https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/documents/language-test.html

Confirmed for implementation:

- Express Entry language results must be less than two years old when completing the profile and when submitting the PR application.
- TCF Canada is listed as an approved French test.

## IRCC Category-Based Selection

URL: https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/express-entry/rounds-invitations/category-based-selection.html

Confirmed for implementation:

- The French-language proficiency category requires French-language test results showing a minimum NCLC score of 7 in all four language abilities.
