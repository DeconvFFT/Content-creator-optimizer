# TCF Canada Exam Targets

## Official Format

| Skill | Format | Time | Training implication |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Listening | 39 QCM questions | 35 min | One-pass audio, fast detail decoding, no replay habit |
| Reading | 39 QCM questions | 60 min | Skim, scan, infer, and protect time |
| Writing | 3 tasks | 60 min | Respect word limits and register |
| Speaking | 3 live tasks | 12 min | Timed interaction, spontaneous structure, recording review |

## Writing Word Limits

- Task 1: 60-120 words.
- Task 2: 120-150 words.
- Task 3: 120-180 words.

## Immigration Training Target

- Minimum category target: NCLC 7 in all four abilities.
- Practical buffer: train toward NCLC 9-style consistency where possible.

## Score Policy

Speaking and writing can use a 20-point training rubric because IRCC publishes production bands on a 20-point scale.

Listening and reading should not be converted from raw score to official TCF score in this system. FEI uses a difficulty-weighted conversion and does not publish the candidate raw-correct count.

## Retake and Validity

- FEI currently lists a 30-day wait between TCF Canada sittings.
- FEI lists the attestation validity as 2 years from issue date.
- IRCC requires Express Entry language results to be less than 2 years old both when completing the profile and submitting the PR application.

Re-check official sources before booking or immigration filing.
